Romeo and Juliet

Act V Quote Quiz

Read questions carefully. Spelling matters!

 
  1. Who speaks these lines?

    Her body sleeps in Capels' monument.
    And her immortal part with angels lives.
    I saw her laid low in her kindred's vault
    And presently took post to tell it you.


  2. To whom are these lines spoken?

    O, I am slain! If thou be merciful.
    Open the tomb, lay me with Juliet.


  3. Who speaks these lines?

    Now must I to the monument alone.
    Within this three hours will fair Juliet wake.
    She will beshrew me much that Romeo
    Hath had no notice of these accidents.


  4. Who speaks these lines?

    Come, come away.
    Thy husband in thy bosom lies dead,
    And Paris, too. Come, I'll dispose of thee
    Among a sisterhood of holy nuns.


  5. To whom are these lines addressed?

    Such mortal drugs I have; but Mantua's law
    Is death to any he that utters them.


    1.   Friar Laurence
    2.   Juliet
    3.   Capulet
  6. Who speaks these lines?

    My poverty but not my will consents.


  7. Who speaks these lines?

    Why art thou yet so fair? Shall I believe
    That unsubstantial Death is amorous,
    And that the lean abhorred monster keeps
    Thee here in dark to be his paramour?


  8. Who speaks these lines?

    Go hence, to have more talk of these sad things;
    Some shall be pardoned, and some punished;
    For never was a story of more woe
    Than this of Juliet and her Romeo.


  9. Who speaks these lines?

    Death, that hath sucked the honey of thy breath,
    Hath had no power yet upon thy beauty.
    Thou art not conquered. Beauty's ensign yet
    Is crimson in thy lips and in thy cheeks,
    And death's pale flag is not advanced there.


    1.   romeo
